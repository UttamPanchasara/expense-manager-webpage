Why Privacy Mode Matters in Finance Apps (And How to Use It)

March 8, 2026 • 6 min read

You're sitting in a coffee shop, checking your expenses on your phone. Someone glances over your shoulder and sees your bank balance, recent purchases, and receipt photos. Uncomfortable, right? This is exactly why Privacy Mode exists in modern expense tracking apps.

What is Privacy Mode?

Privacy Mode is a security feature that instantly hides all sensitive financial information in your expense tracker with a single toggle. When enabled, it conceals:

  • All monetary amounts (balances, expenses, income)
  • Receipt images and attachments
  • Account balances and totals
  • Budget progress and spending insights

You can still navigate the app, view transaction categories, and check dates — but all the sensitive numbers and images remain hidden until you toggle Privacy Mode off.

Why Privacy Mode is Essential

1. Protection in Public Spaces

Whether you're on public transport, in a cafe, or at the office, people can easily see your screen. Privacy Mode prevents shoulder surfing and protects your financial information from prying eyes.

2. Screen Sharing Safety

Need to show someone a feature in your expense tracker during a video call or presentation? Privacy Mode lets you demonstrate the app without exposing your actual financial data.

3. Screenshot Protection

If you need to take screenshots for support or tutorials, Privacy Mode ensures no sensitive amounts or receipts are captured in the image.

4. Family Device Sharing

If family members occasionally use your device, Privacy Mode adds an extra layer of discretion for your personal finances without needing separate user accounts.

How to Enable Privacy Mode

In Expense Manager, enabling Privacy Mode is simple:

  1. 1

    Open the app and go to Settings

  2. 2

    Navigate to Security section

  3. 3

    Toggle Privacy Mode on

  4. 4

    All amounts and receipts are now hidden throughout the app

You can toggle it on and off anytime based on your environment. The setting persists across app restarts, so you don't need to re-enable it every time.

Privacy Mode vs Biometric Lock: What's the Difference?

Privacy Mode

Hides sensitive data while you're using the app

Use when: You're in public and want to check expenses without revealing amounts

Biometric Lock

Prevents unauthorized access to the app entirely

Use when: You want to secure the app from others using your device

Pro Tip: Use both together for maximum security. Biometric lock prevents unauthorized access, while Privacy Mode protects your data when you're actively using the app in public.

Best Practices for Financial Privacy

  • Enable Privacy Mode by default if you frequently check finances in public
  • Combine with biometric lock for comprehensive security
  • Use offline expense trackers to keep data on your device only
  • Be mindful of screen brightness in public to reduce visibility
  • Avoid taking screenshots with Privacy Mode off unless necessary

Conclusion

Privacy Mode is more than just a nice-to-have feature — it's essential for anyone who checks their finances on the go. In a world where we're constantly surrounded by people and cameras, protecting your financial information should be as simple as toggling a switch.

When choosing an expense tracker, make sure Privacy Mode is included. Pair it with an offline expense tracker that keeps all data on your device, and use multi-account tracking to manage all your finances in one private place. Your financial data is personal, and you deserve tools that respect and protect that privacy.
